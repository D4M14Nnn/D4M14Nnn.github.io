The Black Widow

About the Black Widow

The black widow (Latrodectus mactans) is native to parts of North America. It is mostly known for its lethallity, its poison is 15 times stronger than rattlesnake venom.

There are many subspecies with in the black widow species, some include the Eurepean Black Widow, the redback spider, and the western black widow. Black widow spiders are part of the genus (Latrodectus), which also includes brown widow spiders and other spiders that are sometimes called true widows
